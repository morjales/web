Before You Play

Before you start to play, and sometimes before you even make your character, it's a good idea to think of a few things.

What do you want to get out of the game?
Who is going to be the DM?
How hard do you want it to be?

Picking Your Playstyle

The first thing to know about Dungeons and Dragons is that the game is what you make of it.
The game can go from dark and serious to a comedy.
Depending on who you are playing with and how many times you've played, you might want to have different setups.
Some groups play because they can decide to fight a dragon barehanded while others just want a more relaxing game
Choose what kind of experience you want before you set off on your adventure.

Choosing a DM

The DM, or Dungeon Master, is the environment, villains, shopkeepers, weather, and everything in between in your game.
Either you or one of your friends has to take up the difficult job of being the DM in order to play the game.
They are in charge of everything from choosing where your adventure leads, to how certain minor events play out.
You have to remember that when your character dies, they're not coming back, and a bad DM can take a well loved character down very quickly.
Which is why it's always a good idea to bribe them beforehand.
Choose someone who has a knack for storytelling and doesn't have any personal vendettas against the players.
They can make your lives a living hell or shower you with gifts
If you can't come up with a good plot, there are plenty of tools available to make it easier.
In the end, though, it all comes down to what you roll.
